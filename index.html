🎮 Say or Boom – Game Instructions

Type: Face-to-face party game
Players: 2 or more
Platform: Mobile (pass-the-phone style)
Goal: Avoid getting “boomed” by quickly answering before time runs out!

🧠 Objective

Players must name examples based on a prompt (e.g., a category or location) before the bomb timer explodes. You must pass the phone before time runs out. The one holding it when it goes off loses the round.

🕹️ How to Play
  1. Start the Game
    • Open the app and tap “Start”
    • A random prompt/location appears (e.g., “Things you see in a supermarket”)
  2. First Player Responds
    • Player 1 reads the prompt aloud and says a valid answer
    • After answering, pass the phone to the next player
  3. Next Player Answers
    • Player 2 sees the same prompt
    • Must give a different correct answer quickly
    • Then pass the phone again
  4. Tick-Tock... Boom!
    • The timer is ticking secretly in the background
    • The player holding the phone when it explodes (sound/vibration) loses the round
  5. Repeat & Score
    • You can track points manually or just play for fun
    • First to lose 3 times? Do a dare, take a penalty, or just laugh it off!
🎯 Game Highlights
  • Designed for live interaction — no texting, no solo play
  • Encourages quick thinking, verbal creativity, and social bonding
  • Great for parties, classrooms, or family time
  • Aims to reduce screen addiction by promoting active face-to-face gameplay

2021-2022 Game Design Lecture Project

Age of Vehicles

Age of Vehicles Endless Runner Driving Game: This project originated from my longstanding interest in designing games that reflect technological progression. "Age of Vehicles" is a top-down endless runner driving game where players advance through distinct historical eras, each characterized by unique vehicles and collectible items. The gameplay begins in the primitive age, where players operate a wooden car to gather fruits. As the game progresses, players unlock new ages—such as the Primitive Age, featuring 1990s-style vehicles—and ultimately reach the Space Age, each with its own thematic collectibles and challenges.

  • Developed as part of the Game Design curriculum, focusing on the integration of historical context into gameplay mechanics.
  • Implemented advanced wheel physics in Unity, overcoming significant technical challenges to achieve realistic vehicle behavior across different eras.
  • Designed and integrated a robust data backup and save system to ensure persistent player progress and seamless transitions between game stages.
  • Explored procedural content generation to enhance replayability and provide a dynamic player experience.
